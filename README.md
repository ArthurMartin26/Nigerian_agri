# Digital Information & Gendered Agricultural Extension in Nigeria
### Research Project Repository

This repo supports my MSc dissertation project on how **4G mobile network expansion changes the way women farmers in Nigeria access agricultural information**. The focus is on whether digital channels (SMS, WhatsApp, mobile internet) help women bypass male‑dominated, face‑to‑face extension systems.

---

##  Research Questions

1. **Does 4G rollout increase women’s access to digital agricultural information relative to men?**  
2. **Does 4G reduce women’s reliance on traditional extension networks?**  
3. **Does digital substitution improve agricultural decisions, input use, and productivity?**

---

##  Background

Agricultural extension in Nigeria is still heavily gender‑biased, with most agents being men and cultural norms shaping who gets access to advice. Digital information offers a potential workaround.  
4G expansion happened at different times across areas, giving a natural setting to estimate causal effects.

---

##  Data Sources

- **GHS-Panel Section 11 (Extension):** who receives extension, how often, source, cost, usefulness.  
- **GHS-Panel Section 12 (Digital Info):** use of SMS, WhatsApp, apps, internet, topics covered, comfort with tech.  
- **4G Coverage Datasets:** GSMA, Collins, OpenCelliD – for timing and geographic variation.  

All data work will involve merging GHS EA‑level geocode info with external tower coverage datasets.

---

##  Methods

- **Difference‑in‑differences:** staggered 4G rollout at the EA level.  
- **Triple‑difference:** women vs men × treated vs untreated × pre/post.  
- **IV strategy:** engineering‑based predictors of where towers get placed (terrain, line‑of‑sight, distance to fibre backbone).  
- **Within‑household comparisons:** compare men/women inside the same household receiving different information sources.

---

##  Expected Contribution

- Evidence on **gender‑differentiated impacts** of broadband access.  
- Understanding how **digital information substitutes for traditional extension**.  
- Policy relevance for designing **gender‑responsive digital agriculture strategies** in LMICs.
